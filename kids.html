The World Through Time, for Children

The same journey, from the first people to 1945, told for younger readers. Bigger pictures, shorter stories, and one extra companion the grown-up version does not have: the animals.

Every age in this history has its animals. The dog who hunted beside the first people. The donkey who carried the world's first trade. The camel who turned the deserts into roads. The horse who pulled the chariots of Pharaoh. The elephant who walked from Baghdad to Germany as a gift. The pigeons who carried the post across an empire. They are all being written down now, age by age, and they will guide children through the whole story.
