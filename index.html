The World Through Time

A timeline of the whole world, from the first people to the end of the Second World War, running on two rails. The gold rail is the Long Thread: one continuous story, from Adam through Abraham, Moses, Jesus and Muhammad, to the civilisations that grew out of them. The silver rail is the Wider World: everyone else who was alive at the same moment, from Egypt and China to Athens, Rome, Cahokia, Great Zimbabwe and the navigators of the Pacific. Where two entries sit facing each other across the rails, those things were happening at the same time.

Click any entry and it opens the map of that exact age, with the borders as they really were and not as they are now. Hover over any land and it tells you what state held it then. Gold pins mark the places in the story, each with the name it had at the time, the name it has today, and its coordinates. Every age also records what was invented, which animals people lived and worked with, what they spoke, what they used for money, who held power, and a moral ledger that names the worst injustices of that age and its fairest societies, whoever they happened to be.

Why it exists

Because reading history should not mean losing your place on the map. If you have ever read about a prophet, a battle or an empire and found yourself wondering where exactly that was, what the place is called now, and what on earth was going on everywhere else at the time, then this was built for you. It assumes no background knowledge and no particular faith. Religious words are explained in plain English. Nothing before roughly 2000 BC is given a year, because the sources give the order of events and not the dates. Where the Islamic sources and the wider historical record disagree, this site follows the Islamic sources and tells you so rather than blurring it. And injustice is named wherever it happened, by whoever did it.
