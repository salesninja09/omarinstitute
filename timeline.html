The World Through Time

“There is a lesson in the stories of such people for those who understand.”

Quran 12:111

Two rails run side by side. The gold rail is the Long Thread: one continuous story, from the first people, through Abraham, Moses, Jesus and Muhammad, to the empires that grew out of them and the day the last caliph left on a train. The silver rail is the Wider World: everyone else who was alive at the same moment. Egypt and China, Athens and Rome, Baghdad and Cahokia, the Mongols and the Pacific navigators. Open any entry and you get the map of that exact age, and you can see what each place was called then and what it is called now.

How to use this timeline

1Scroll the two rails. Gold entries on the right follow The Long Thread. Silver entries on the left follow The Wider World. When two entries sit side by side, those things were happening at the same time.
2Click any entry. It opens the world map of that era, with the borders as they actually were, not as they are now.
3Hover the map. Any territory will tell you who held it at that moment. Gold pins mark the places in the story, with the name it had then, the name it has now, and its coordinates.
4Open the panels. Every era tells you what people spoke, what they used for money, what they invented, which animals lived alongside them, and a Moral Ledger naming that age's worst injustices and its fairest societies, whoever they were.

You need no background knowledge and no particular faith to read this. Religious and historical terms are explained in ordinary English with the original word in brackets. Eras that our sources do not date are left undated rather than filled with invented years. The Islamic sources lead on the gold rail, standard scholarship leads on the silver one, and where they disagree the disagreement is stated rather than hidden.

Where all of this comes from

Maps are drawn from the Historical Basemaps dataset and simplified for the web. Ancient borders were zones rather than lines, and no map of them can be exact.
